About Me

I am an adjuct research professor at UNC Chapel Hill. Most of my work involves intracete calculations and visualizations that help further the understanding of the interior of the Earth. If you think this sounds too technical and boring to you than it may come as a surprise if I tell you that my work also involves travelling to places and working out in the wilderness where no man has set a foot on (like Antarctica for instance...). Yes, some may think this is way too cool but nothing comes for free. The fieldwork that I carry out sometimes requires to carry out some laborious activities such as diging 6 feet deep holes in the ground, mixing and pouring concrete and carrying 80 lbs concrtete bags up the mountains. Well... and it is totally worth it :)

I have never imagened I would enjoy playing pick-a-boo as a grown man this much...In addition to the passion for my family, I am also consumed with computer programming and coding.
